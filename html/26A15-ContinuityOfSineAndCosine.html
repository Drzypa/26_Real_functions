continuity of sine and cosine


Theorem.  The real functions  x↦sin⁡x  and  x↦cos⁡x  are continuous at every real number x.

Proof.  Let ε be an arbitrary positive number.  Denote  Δsinx=:sinz-sinx,  Δcosx=:cosz-cosx  where we suppose that  |z-x|<π2.  We may interpret |z-x| as an arc of the unit circle of the x⁢y-plane.  Let’s think in the circle the right triangle with hypotenuse the chord of the arc and the catheti (i.e. the shorter sides) vertical and horizontal.  Then |Δ⁢sin⁡x| and |Δ⁢cos⁡x| are just these cathets; so we have

|Δ⁢sin⁡x|≦|z-x|,|Δ⁢cos⁡x|≦|z-x|.

If we make  |z-x|<ε,  then also  |Δ⁢sin⁡x| and |Δ⁢cos⁡x| are less than ε.  It means that both functions are continuous at x.
