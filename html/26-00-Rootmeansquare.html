root-mean-square


If x1,x2,…,xn are real numbers, we define their root-mean-square or quadratic mean as

R⁢(x1,x2,…,xn)=x12+x22+⋯+xn2n.

The root-mean-square of a random variable X is defined as the square root of the expectation of X2:

R⁢(X)=E⁢(X2)

If X1,X2,…,Xn are random variables with standard deviations σ1,σ2,…,σn, then the standard deviation of their arithmetic mean, X1+X2+⋯+Xnn, is the root-mean-square of σ1,σ2,…,σn.
