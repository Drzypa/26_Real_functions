derivation of geometric mean as the limit of the power mean


Fix x1,x2,…,xn∈ℝ+. Then let

μ⁢(r):=(x1r+⋯+xnrn)1/r.

For r≠0, by definition μ⁢(r) is the rth power mean of the xi. It is also clear that μ⁢(r) is a differentiable function for r≠0. What is limr→0⁡μ⁢(r)?

We will first calculate limr→0⁡log⁡μ⁢(r) using l’Hôpital’s rule (http://planetmath.org/LHpitalsRule).

limr→0⁡log⁡μ⁢(r) =limr→0⁡log⁡(x1r+⋯+xnrn)r
=limr→0⁡(x1r⁢log⁡x1+⋯+xnr⁢log⁡xnn)(x1r+⋯+xnrn)
=limr→0⁡x1r⁢log⁡x1+⋯+xnr⁢log⁡xnx1r+⋯+xnr
=log⁡x1+⋯+log⁡xnn
=log⁡x1⁢⋯⁢xnn.

It follows immediately that

limr→0⁡(x1r+⋯+xnrn)1/r=x1⁢⋯⁢xnn.
