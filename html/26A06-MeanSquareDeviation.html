mean square deviation


If f is a Riemann integrable real function on the interval  [a,b] which is wished to be approximated by another function φ with the same property, then the mean (http://planetmath.org/MeanValueTheorem)

m=1b-a⁢∫ab[f⁢(x)-φ⁢(x)]2⁢𝑑x

is called the mean square deviation of φ from f.

For example, if  sin⁡x  is approximated by x on  [0,π2], the mean square deviation is

2π⁢∫0π2(sin⁡x-x)2⁢𝑑x≈ 0.04923.
