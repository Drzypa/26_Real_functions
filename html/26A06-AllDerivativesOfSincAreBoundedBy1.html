all derivatives of sinc are bounded by 1


Let us show that all derivatives of sinc are bounded by 1.

First of all, let us out that sinc⁡(t)≤1 is bounded by the Jordan’s inequality. To the derivatives, let us write sinc as a Fourier integral,

sinc⁡(t)=12⁢∫-11ei⁢x⁢t⁢𝑑x.

Let k=1,2,…. Then

dkd⁢tk⁢sinc⁡(t)=12⁢∫-11(i⁢x)k⁢ei⁢x⁢t⁢𝑑x.

and

|dkd⁢tk⁢sinc⁡(t)| = |12⁢∫-11(i⁢x)k⁢ei⁢x⁢t⁢𝑑x|
≤ 12⁢∫-11|(i⁢x)k⁢ei⁢x⁢t|⁢𝑑x
≤ 12⁢∫-11|x|k⁢𝑑x
≤ 12⋅2⁢∫01|x|k⁢𝑑x
≤ ∫01xk⁢𝑑x
≤ 1k+1
< 1.
