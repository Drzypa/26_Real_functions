another proof of Young inequality


Let

F⁢(x)=∫0xϕ⁢(t)⁢𝑑t, and ⁢G⁢(x)=∫0xϕ-1⁢(t)⁢𝑑t.

Since ϕ-1 is strictly increasing, G is strictly convex, hence lies above its supporting line, i.e. for every c and x≠c

G⁢(b)>G⁢(c)+G′⁢(c)⁢(b-c)=G⁢(c)+ϕ-1⁢(c)⁢(b-c).

In particular, for c=ϕ⁢(a) we have

F⁢(a)+G⁢(b)>F⁢(a)+G⁢(ϕ⁢(a))+a⁢(b-ϕ⁢(a))=a⁢b,

because F⁢(a)+G⁢(ϕ⁢(a))=a⁢ϕ⁢(a).
