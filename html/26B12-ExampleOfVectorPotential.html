example of vector potential


If the solenoidal vector  U→=U→⁢(x,y,z)  is a homogeneous function of degree λ (≠-2),  then it has the vector potential

A→=1λ+2⁢U→×r→, (1)

where  r→=x⁢i→+y⁢j→+z⁢k→  is the position vector.

Proof.  Using the entry nabla acting on products, we first may write

∇×(1λ+2⁢U→×r→)=1λ+2⁢[(r→⋅∇)⁢U→-(U→⋅∇)⁢r→-(∇⋅U→)⁢r→+(∇⋅r→)⁢U→].

In the brackets the first product is, according to Euler’s theorem on homogeneous functions, equal to λ⁢U→.  The second product can be written as  Ux⁢∂⁡r→∂⁡x+Uy⁢∂⁡r→∂⁡y+Uz⁢∂⁡r→∂⁡z, which is Ux⁢i→+Uy⁢j→+Uz⁢k→, i.e. U→.  The third product is, due to the sodenoidalness, equal to  0⁢r→=0→.  The last product equals to 3⁢U→ (see the first formula (http://planetmath.org/PositionVector) for position vector).  Thus we get the result

∇×(1λ+2⁢U→×r→)=1λ+2⁢[λ⁢U→-U→-0→+3⁢U→]=U→.

This means that U→ has the vector potential (1).
