proof of quotient rule


Let F⁢(x)=f⁢(x)/g⁢(x). Then

F′⁢(x) = limh→0⁡F⁢(x+h)-F⁢(x)h=limh→0⁡f⁢(x+h)g⁢(x+h)-f⁢(x)g⁢(x)h
= limh→0⁡f⁢(x+h)⁢g⁢(x)-f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x+h)h⁢g⁢(x+h)⁢g⁢(x)

Like the product rule, the key to this proof is subtracting and adding the same quantity. We separate f and g in the above expression by subtracting and adding the term f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x) in the numerator.

F′⁢(x) = limh→0⁡f⁢(x+h)⁢g⁢(x)-f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)+f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)-f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x+h)h⁢g⁢(x+h)⁢g⁢(x)
= limh→0⁡g⁢(x)⁢f⁢(x+h)-f⁢(x)h-f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x+h)-g⁢(x)hg⁢(x+h)⁢g⁢(x)
= limh→0⁡g⁢(x)⋅limh→0⁡f⁢(x+h)-f⁢(x)h-limh→0⁡f⁢(x)⋅limh→0⁡g⁢(x+h)-g⁢(x)hlimh→0⁡g⁢(x+h)⋅limh→0⁡g⁢(x)
= g⁢(x)⁢f′⁢(x)-f⁢(x)⁢g′⁢(x)[g⁢(x)]2
