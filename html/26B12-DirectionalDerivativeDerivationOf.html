directional derivative, derivation of


Let f⁢(x,y) be a function where x=x⁢(t) and y=y⁢(t). Let r→=a⁢𝐢^+b⁢𝐣^ be the vector in the desired direction. The line through this vector is given parametrically by:

x=x0+a⁢t;y=y0+b⁢t

The derivative of f with respect to t is as follows:

∂⁡f∂⁡t=∂⁡f∂⁡x⁢d⁢xd⁢t+∂⁡f∂⁡y⁢d⁢yd⁢t

But from the equation of the line, we know that d⁢xd⁢t=a and d⁢yd⁢t=b so the derivative becomes:

∂⁡f∂⁡t=∂⁡f∂⁡x⁢a+∂⁡f∂⁡y⁢b=∇⁡f⋅r→
