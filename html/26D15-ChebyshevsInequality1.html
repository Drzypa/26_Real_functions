Chebyshev’s inequality


If x1,x2,…,xn and y1,y2,…,yn are two sequences (at least one of them consisting of positive numbers):

  • •

    if x1<x2<⋯<xn and y1<y2<⋯<yn then

    (x1+x2+⋯+xnn)⁢(y1+y2+⋯+ynn)≤x1⁢y1+x2⁢y2+⋯+xn⁢ynn.
  • •

    if x1<x2<⋯<xn and y1>y2>⋯>yn then

    (x1+x2+⋯+xnn)⁢(y1+y2+⋯+ynn)≥x1⁢y1+x2⁢y2+⋯+xn⁢ynn.
