example of integral mean value theorem


Example.

If f is a continuous real function on an interval [a,b], then there exists a ζ∈(a,b) such that

∫abf⁢(x)⁢dx=f⁢(ζ)⁢(b-a).
Proof.

Let g⁢(x)≡1. Then by the Integral Mean Value Theorem, there exists ζ∈(a,b) such that

∫abf⁢(x)⁢dx =∫abf⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)⁢dx
=f⁢(ζ)⁢∫abg⁢(x)⁢dx
=f⁢(ζ)⁢∫ab1⁢dx
=f⁢(ζ)⁢(b-a)

as required. ∎
