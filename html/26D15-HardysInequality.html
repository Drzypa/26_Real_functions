Hardy’s inequality


Suppose p>1 and {an} is a sequence of nonnegative real numbers. Let An=∑i=1nai. Then

∑n≥1(Ann)p<(pp-1)p⁢∑n≥1anp,

unless all the an are zero. The constant is best possible.

This theorem has an integral analogue: Suppose that p>1 and f≥0 on (0,∞). Let F⁢(x)=∫0xf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t. Then

∫0∞(Fx)p⁢𝑑x<(pp-1)p⁢∫0∞fp⁢(x)⁢𝑑x,

unless f≡0. The constant is best possible.
