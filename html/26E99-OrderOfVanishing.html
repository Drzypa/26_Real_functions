order of vanishing


Definition.  Let x0 be a zero (http://planetmath.org/ZeroOfAFunction) of the real function Δ.  The order of vanishing of Δ at x0 is n, if  limx→x0⁡Δ⁢(x)xn has a non-zero finite value.

Usually, x0 of the definition is 0.

Example.  If the curves   y=f⁢(x)  and  y=g⁢(x)  have in the point  (x0,y0)  the order of contact n, then the difference   Δ⁢(h):=g⁢(x0+h)-f⁢(x0+h)  of the ordinates has n+1-order of vanishing.
