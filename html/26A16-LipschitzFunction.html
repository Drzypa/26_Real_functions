Lipschitz function


Let W⊆X⊆ℂ and f:X→ℂ. Then f is on W if there exists an M∈ℝ such that, for all x,y∈W, x≠y

|f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)|≤M⁢|x-y|

If a,b∈ℝ with a<b and f:[a,b]→ℝ is Lipschitz on (a,b), then f is absolutely continuous on [a,b].

Example: Is

f⁢(x)=1x,x∈[0,1]

a Lipschitz function.

We need to estimate the constant M.

|f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)|=|1x-1y|=|x-yx⁢y|=|x-yx⁢y⁢(x+y)|=1|x⁢y⁢(x+y)|⁢|x-y|.

It follows that

M=1|x⁢y⁢(x+y)|

and f⁢(x) is not Lipschitz at x=0.
