one-sided continuity


The real function f is continuous from the left in the point  x=x0  iff

limx→x0-⁡f⁢(x)=f⁢(x0).

The real function f is continuous from the right in the point  x=x0  iff

limx→x0+⁡f⁢(x)=f⁢(x0).

The real function f is continuous on the closed interval  [a,b]  iff it is continuous at all points of the open interval  (a,b),  from the right continuous at a and from the left continuous at b.

Examples.  The ceiling function ⌈x⌉ is from the left continuous at each integer, the mantissa function x-⌊x⌋ is from the right continuous at each integer.
