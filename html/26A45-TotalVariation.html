total variation


Let γ:[a,b]→X be a function mapping an interval [a,b] to a metric space (X,d). We say that γ is of bounded variation if there is a constant M such that, for each partition P={a=t0<t1<⋯<tn=b} of [a,b],

v⁢(γ,P)=∑k=1nd⁢(γ⁢(tk),γ⁢(tk-1))≤M.

The total variation Vγ of γ is defined by

Vγ=sup⁡{v⁢(γ,P):P is a partition of [a,b]}.

It can be shown that, if X is either ℝ or ℂ, every continuously differentiable (or piecewise continuously differentiable) function γ:[a,b]→X is of bounded variation (http://planetmath.org/ContinuousDerivativeImpliesBoundedVariation), and

Vγ=∫ab|γ′⁢(t)|⁢𝑑t.

Also, if γ is of bounded variation and f:[a,b]→X is continuous, then the Riemann-Stieltjes integral ∫abf⁢𝑑γ is finite.

If γ is also continuous, it is said to be a rectifiable path, and V⁢(γ) is the length of its trace.

If X=ℝ, it can be shown that γ is of bounded variation if and only if it is the difference of two monotonic functions.
