proof of Barbalat’s lemma


We suppose that y⁢(t)↛0 as t→∞. There exists a sequence (tn) in ℝ+ such that tn→∞ as n→∞ and |y⁢(tn)|≥ε for all n∈ℕ. By the uniform continuity of y, there exists a δ>0 such that, for all n∈ℕ and all t∈ℝ,

|tn-t|≤δ⇒|y⁢(tn)-y⁢(t)|≤ε2.

So, for all t∈[tn,tn+δ], and for all n∈ℕ we have

|y⁢(t)| = |y⁢(tn)-(y⁢(tn)-y⁢(t))|≥|y⁢(tn)|-|y⁢(tn)-y⁢(t)|≥
≥ ε-ε2=ε2.

Therefore,

|∫0tn+δy⁢(t)⁢𝑑t-∫0tny⁢(t)⁢𝑑t|=|∫tntn+δy⁢(t)⁢𝑑t|=∫tntn+δ|y⁢(t)|⁢𝑑t≥ε⁢δ2>0

for each n∈ℕ. By the hypothesis, the improprer Riemann integral ∫0∞y⁢(t)⁢𝑑t exists, and thus the left hand side of the inequality converges to 0 as n→∞, yielding a contradiction.
