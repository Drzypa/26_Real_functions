proof of Fermat’s Theorem (stationary points)


Suppose that x0 is a local maximum (a similar proof applies if x0 is a local minimum). Then there exists δ>0 such that (x0-δ,x0+δ)⊂(a,b) and such that we have f⁢(x0)≥f⁢(x) for all x with |x-x0|<δ. Hence for h∈(0,δ) we notice that it holds

f⁢(x0+h)-f⁢(x0)h≤0.

Since the limit of this ratio as h→0+ exists and is equal to f′⁢(x0) we conclude that f′⁢(x0)≤0. On the other hand for h∈(-δ,0) we notice that

f⁢(x0+h)-f⁢(x0)h≥0

but again the limit as h→0+ exists and is equal to f′⁢(x0) so we also have f′⁢(x0)≥0.

Hence we conclude that f′⁢(x0)=0.

To prove the second part of the statement (when x0 is equal to a or b), just notice that in such points we have only one of the two estimates written above.
