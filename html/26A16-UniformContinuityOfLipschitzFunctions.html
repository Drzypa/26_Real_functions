uniform continuity of Lipschitz functions


Proposition 1.

An Hölder continuous mapping is uniformly continuous. In particular any Lipschitz continuous mapping is uniformly continuous.

Proof.

Let f:X→Y be a mapping such that for some C>0 and α with 0<α≤1 one has

dY⁢(f⁢(p),f⁢(q))≤C⁢dX⁢(p,q)α.

For every given ϵ>0, choose δ=(ϵ/(C+1))1α. If p,q∈X are given points satisfying

dX⁢(p,q)<δ

then

dY⁢(f⁢(p),f⁢(q))≤C⁢δα≤C⁢ϵC+1<ϵ,

as desired. ∎
